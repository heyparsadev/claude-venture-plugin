# Mom Test Rules (Rob Fitzpatrick) — Condensed

The test: your questions should yield truth **even from your mom** — someone who loves you and will lie to protect your feelings. Everyone lies politely when you pitch; nobody lies about their own past.

## The three rules

1. **Talk about their life, not your idea.** The moment you pitch, every answer after it is contaminated.
2. **Ask about specifics in the past, not opinions about the future.** "Tell me about the last time X happened" beats "would you ever...?"
3. **Listen for behavior and commitment, not compliments.**

## Question transforms

| ❌ Banned (invites lies) | ✅ Ask instead |
|---|---|
| "Would you buy a product that did X?" | "How do you deal with X today? What does it cost you?" |
| "Do you think this is a good idea?" | "Why did you last try to solve this? What happened?" |
| "Would you pay $Y?" | "What are you paying now to handle this? (tools, staff time, losses)" |
| "Is X a big problem for you?" | "When did X last bite you? Walk me through it." |
| "What features would you want?" | "What have you cobbled together yourself? Show me." |

Persian equivalents of the killer openers:
- «آخرین باری که این مشکل برات پیش اومد رو تعریف کن — دقیقاً چی شد؟»
- «الان چطوری حلش می‌کنی؟ چقدر وقت/پول می‌بره؟»
- «تا حالا دنبال راه‌حلش گشتی؟ چی پیدا کردی، چرا نموندی روش؟»

## Reading the answers

**Strong signals (count these):** they already pay money or hours for a workaround; they tried to build/buy a fix; they get emotional retelling it; they introduce you to others unprompted; they ask "when can I have it?" *and accept a concrete commitment*.

**Noise (count as zero):** "sounds great / خیلی خوبه", "I'd definitely use it", generic feature wishes, advice about what "people" would want.

**Commitment ladder (end every promising interview one rung up):** another meeting with calendar time → intro to 2 peers → access to their data/workflow → letter of intent / pre-order / pilot payment. Refusal to climb = polite no, record it as such.

## Common interviewer failures

- Pitching in minute one ("we're building...") — say instead: "I'm researching how <segment> handles <job>; I have nothing to sell."
- Leading: "don't you find X frustrating?" → "walk me through how X goes."
- Hearing what you hoped: write the quote verbatim *then* interpret.
- Only interviewing fans/friends — note sourcing honestly in synthesis.
