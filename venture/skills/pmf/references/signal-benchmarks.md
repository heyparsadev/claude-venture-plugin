# PMF Signal Benchmarks by Business Type

Directional industry rules of thumb (sources: public writings of a16z, Lenny Rachitsky's benchmark surveys, OpenView SaaS benchmarks, Sean Ellis). Use as thresholds in the scorecard; cite as "industry rule of thumb" — and adjust expectations for market maturity (emerging-market consumer behavior differs; note when you do).

## B2B SaaS

| Signal | Weak | Decent | Strong |
|---|---|---|---|
| Logo retention @ month 12 | <70% | 80–90% | >90% |
| Net revenue retention | <90% | 100–110% | >120% |
| Sean Ellis very-disappointed | <25% | 25–40% | >40% |
| Sales cycle trend | lengthening | stable | shortening + inbound appearing |
| Usage | logins only | weekly active teams | daily active, workflow-embedded |

## Consumer app (B2C)

| Signal | Weak | Decent | Strong |
|---|---|---|---|
| D1 / D7 / D30 retention | <20/10/5% | 30/15/8% | >40/20/10% |
| Retention curve | decays to ~0 | flattens late | flattens early at meaningful % |
| Organic share of installs | <20% | 30–50% | >50% |
| DAU/MAU | <10% | 15–25% | >25% (habit) |

## Marketplace

| Signal | Weak | Decent | Strong |
|---|---|---|---|
| Repeat purchase rate (buyer, 90d) | <20% | 30–40% | >50% |
| Liquidity (fill/match rate) | <30% | 50–70% | >80% |
| Supply retention @ month 6 | <40% | 50–70% | >70% |
| Cross-side organic growth | none | one side | both sides referring |

## Service business / agency

| Signal | Weak | Decent | Strong |
|---|---|---|---|
| Client retention / renewal | <50% | 60–80% | >80% + expansion |
| Referral share of new clients | <10% | 20–40% | >40% |
| Pricing power | discounting to win | holding rates | raising rates, waitlist |

## E-commerce / D2C

| Signal | Weak | Decent | Strong |
|---|---|---|---|
| 90-day repeat purchase | <15% | 20–30% | >35% |
| Contribution margin after CAC, 1st order | deeply negative | ~breakeven | positive |
| Review velocity & rating | sparse/<4.0 | steady/4.0–4.5 | accelerating/>4.5 |

## Universal qualitative tells

**Strong:** users complain loudly during outages; they invite colleagues unprompted; they stretch the product beyond intended use; churn interviews say "budget", not "didn't need it".
**Weak:** polite praise but no usage; "I'll use it when..."; activation requires hand-holding every time; discounts drive all conversion.
