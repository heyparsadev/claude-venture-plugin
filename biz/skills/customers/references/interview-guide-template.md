# Interview Guide Template

Generate in the **customers' language**. 15–20 minutes. Customize bracketed parts from the venture's riskiest assumptions — every assumption gets ≥1 question.

```markdown
# Interview Guide — <Venture> · v<N>
<date> · target: <ICP hypothesis> · assumptions under test: <#1, #2...>

## Intro script (verbatim)
"<Hi, I'm ... — I'm researching how <segment> handles <job-to-be-done>.
Not selling anything; there may not even be a product. I want to learn how it works
for you today. OK if I take notes?>"

## Warm-up (context, 2 min)
1. <Tell me about your <role/business> — size, how long, daily rhythm?>

## Core — past behavior on the problem (10 min)
2. <Walk me through the last time <problem situation> happened. What did you do, step by step?>
3. <How often does that happen? What does each occurrence cost you — time, money, stress?>
   ↳ probe: <can you put a number on it? last month for example?>
4. <What do you use today to handle it? (tool, person, paper, nothing)>
   ↳ probe: <what do you pay for that? what's most annoying about it?>
5. <Have you ever looked for or tried something better? What? Why didn't it stick?>
6. <If assumption-specific: e.g. "who decides on buying tools like this in your clinic?">
7. <What's the most painful part of <broader workflow> overall?> *(open door — often reveals the REAL problem)*

## Ranking (2 min)
8. <Of the things we discussed — which one, if it vanished tomorrow, would change your week most?>

## Closing — commitment ladder (3 min)
9. <Can I follow up in a few weeks when I know more?> → calendar
10. <Who else do you know dealing with this? Could you intro me to 1–2?>
11. <(if product exists) Want early access — and would you walk through it with me on a call?>

## DO NOT
- pitch the idea, mention features, or ask "would you use/pay/like".
- accept compliments as answers — redirect to "tell me about the last time...".
```
